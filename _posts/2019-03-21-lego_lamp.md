# LEGO project: Lamp

At work I recently got a new [desk lamp](https://www.amazon.fr/gp/product/B074DW24KX/ref=ppx_yo_dt_b_asin_title_o01_s00?ie=UTF8&psc=1):

![](/assets/lego/lamp/lamp.png)

The lamp is designed in a way that emits light not only toward the desk surface but also on the sides. I find it uncomfortable to have light shining directly toward my eyes.

LEGO to the rescue!


## The project

The goal of my LEGO « lamp » project is to design something that can block the
light coming from the side of the lamp, without blocking the light coming from
the bottom.

The lamp is quite strong so weight is not really an issue as long as we do not
use bricks made of lead.

Ideally it should blend in the general look and feel of the office environement.
It should not catch the eye too much to avoid being distracted (I am one of
those persons who can get crazy if they have something prominent in their field
of view).

Ease of transportation and setup is not critical, but the simpler the better!


## Before we start

First thing to do is get the lamp's dimensions. I took a piece of paper and drew
marks to get the size of the inner and outer circle. I edited the picture below
to show how the lamp was when I drew the marks.

![](/assets/lego/lamp/initial_measurments.JPG)


## The first prototype

Then I built the very first prototype. It was a simple square box with brick
walls and some plates to cover the top.
[Rainbow warrior](https://www.brothers-brick.com/lego-glossary/#Rainbow-Warrior) style.
There also needed to be an opening to accomodate the lamp's support arm.

(I actually never took the measurments of the support arm's implantation point.
I never focused on it, I just adapted the design around it without really
thinking about it.)

The box allowed me to determine that the lamp had a diameter of between 14 and
16 studs. Basically, 16 studs is the smallest even number of studs that is
larger than the lamp. Also, 4 bricks covers the entire height.


## First improvements

With that precious information in hand, I modified the ugly box and tried to
approximate a circle the best I could using 1x6, 1x4, 1x3, 1x2 and 1x1 bricks.

![](/assets/lego/lamp/brick_circle.png)

I also designed a proper top cover using
[wedge plates](https://rebrickable.com/parts/30503/wedge-plate-4-x-4-cut-corner/),
and tried to give it a clean and symetric look. Note the extra purely decorative
[1x1 round tiles](https://rebrickable.com/parts/98138/tile-round-1-x-1/) in the
corners.

![](/assets/lego/lamp/top_lid.png)

This was looking quite good, but could be easier to setup. I had to undo the
top cover to setup the brick wall and then assemble both parts around the lamp.

But it worked quite well!


## Center opening

Next I wanted to try and make an opening in the center, to try and preserve
the original "donut" spirit of the lamp.

Turns out the inner circle is between 8 and 10 studs wide, so 8 studs is the
maximum that can fit in the empty space. Again I tried to approximate a cricle
and came up with this:

![](/assets/lego/lamp/brick_core.png)

I later realized that the 4x4 macaroni pieces are perfect for that, although
there is no proper way to make something sturdy without introducing other parts:

![](/assets/lego/lamp/macaroni_core.png)

I reviewed the top cover to do the corresponding opening:

![](/assets/lego/lamp/top_lid_open.png)

The center opening made the setup process even worse than before.


## Striving for minimalism

Some time later I noticed that the usual orientation of the lamp makes it so 
that blocking the light leaking through the center opening is not required.

The whole top lid is in fact mostly useless. Its only use was to prevent the
side walls from falling down, but this can be achieved with only a handfull
of strategically placed 2x2 plates.

The 4 studs high brick wall can actually be only 3 studs high, as the lamp only
emits light from roughly the bottom half section.

So I removed as much pieces as I could and left only what was strictly required.

![](/assets/lego/lamp/final1.jpg)
![](/assets/lego/lamp/final2.jpg)
![](/assets/lego/lamp/final3.jpg)

Incidentally, this design is extremely easy to setup and take off: lift it up a
bit, rotate 90 degres, and slide it out! Could not be easier.


## This is only the beginning

This last design is now setup permanently on my desk. It achieves its mission
perfectly, but I am not crazy about the way it looks.

I am exploring other designs, some of them quite exotic. Stay tuned for further
updates!
