# First came the watch

I am the lucky owner of an [Apple Watch series 4](https://www.apple.com/apple-watch-series-4/).
I bought it last year just when it came out. This is my first Apple Watch, and my
first wrist watch in a long time. And I love it.

The Apple Watch comes with a [variety of watch bands](https://www.apple.com/shop/watch/bands) that you can choose from.
The bands can be easily attached and removed from the watch using a simple slide-and-latch system.

Every version of the Apple Watch comes with a different band.
Mine came with a simple "fluoroelastomer with a pin‑and‑tuck closure" aka sport band.
Kind of like this one:

![](/assets/lego/apple_watch_band/apple_store_sport_band.png)

Apple Watch bands are a huge fashion element, and even before I received mine
I was already thinking about the kind of bands that would suite me best.

And I quickly asked my self: "Could I make a watch band out of LEGO?"


# Then came the thinking process

## Attaching to the watch

First thing I needed was a way to attach my LEGO band to the watch.
Having LEGO pieces attach directly to the watch was obviously not possible.

Amazon to the rescue 🚀
I found those [little things](https://www.amazon.fr/gp/product/B07F36XFND/ref=ppx_yo_dt_b_asin_title_o01_s01?ie=UTF8&psc=1),
designed to make custom Apple Watch bands.
Exactly what I needed! They event come with a handy tool to spring out the silver bar and put it back again.

![](/assets/lego/apple_watch_band/watch_clips.png)

## Attaching to the thing that attaches to the watch

Now I needed to find a way to attach LEGO pieces to these clips.
First thing I tried was those plates with a ring attached to it:

![](/assets/lego/apple_watch_band/lego_clip.png)

I could fit the bar in it and it worked quite well.

## A first design idea

I initially thought about using clip pieces to make the band,

somehing like this:

![](/assets/lego/apple_watch_band/clip_design.png)

But the clip pieces are not very strong. They hold well if you don't pull to hard on it,
but bumping into furniture or door frames could easily pull them apart.

This was not acceptable as the idea of the band breaking and the Apple Watch flying away is just too scary for me 😱.
Plus the large plates did not look very comfortable to wear all day long.

## A better design

I finally used technic beams like this one:

![](/assets/lego/apple_watch_band/lego_beam.png)

Here is what it looks like:

![](/assets/lego/apple_watch_band/final1.jpg)

Here is a better view of the watch alone:

![](/assets/lego/apple_watch_band/final0.jpg)

I used 3L technic pins to make three staggered rows. This gives a very flexible
yet strong structure that is quite comfortable to wear.
Here is the LEGO band next to the band that came with my watch:

![](/assets/lego/apple_watch_band/bands.jpg)

Here is a look on how the pieces are put together:

![](/assets/lego/apple_watch_band/final5.jpg)

The piece on the left shows the blue technic pins and how the beams fit together.
The piece on the right shows the basic element that forms the band.
I attached as many segments as needed and adjusted the length to fit my wrist.

The large holes in the beam pieces can fit the bars from the watch clips, and the clips fit 3 pieces perfectly!
I used a longer beam (3L instead of 2L) at each end to give a clean look.

Here is a closer look on the ends. You can see how the LEGO pieces attach
to the watch clip.

![](/assets/lego/apple_watch_band/final4.jpg)

And here is a very cool picture 😎:

![](/assets/lego/apple_watch_band/final2.jpg)

Toward the middle of the band I used a normal pin instead of a 3L to provide an easy way to open the band:

![](/assets/lego/apple_watch_band/final3.jpg)

Just rotate a beam out of the way and disengage. Easy and yet strong enough.

Cool thing with this design is you can play with colors. The pins come only on black and blue,
but the beams come in a lot of different colors.

I like the modern and clean look of blue and black. I configured my watch face
to use the same blue color and looks really nice (you can see it on the first picture above).


## Conclusion

My LEGO Apple Watch band is definitly unique and gives my Apple Watch a very distinctive look
that communicates my passion for LEGO.

With LEGO, you can really make anything. And that is awesome.

Stay tuned for more LEGO adventures!
